    • ABOUT ME

    M y name is Keegan Clendenin, and I am from Columbia, South Carolina. I am currently pursuing a double major in Finance and International Business with a minor in French at the University of South Carolina, where I will graduate in May 2025 with a Leadership Distinction in Global Learning.

    In the Fall of 2023, I had the opportunity to study abroad at ESSEC Business School in France, where I focused on sustainability, leadership, and ethics in global business. This experience not only strengthened my language skills and cultural competency, but also reaffirmed my interest in the intersection of business, policy, and global impact. I’ve always had a deep curiosity about how organizations function within broader economic and socio-political systems—and how we, as future leaders, can drive meaningful and responsible change.

    During my Sophomore year, I became a member of the Gamecock Consulting Club, where I quickly assumed a leadership role in recruiting and preparing new members. I hosted casing workshops, offered mock interviews, and guided peers through their first client projects. That same year, I worked on a consulting project with Squadron Ethos, a startup company hoping to monetise a niche community of aviation enthusiasts in South Carolina. I also collaborated with ISI Consulting as a student consultant, providing advice on how to leverage their existing assets and service team to put more projects in their pipeline.

    M y junior year brought new challenges and learning opportunities as I explored finance more deeply through a role at Capital City Financial Partners. Working under a CPA and financial advisor, I conducted stock evaluations, participated in tax consultations, and prepared for client- facing responsibilities.

    These experiences, alongside my coursework, have helped shape the professional I strive to become. As you read on, you will find that my “Key Insights” include:

    •The Value of Multiculturalism in High-Level Corporate Strategy

    •Sometimes You Have to Meet People on Their Terms

    •Global Stakeholder Management in Assessing Innovation Strategies in Multinational Corporations

    A dditionally, my time studying International Business and French has reminded me that effective leadership means more than delivering results—it’s about empathy, active listening, and resilience. In the future, I hope to merge my passions for financial strategy, international policy, and ethical business practices to lead with purpose in a global arena.

    As I have navigated work in sales, legal services, accounting, and construction, I’ve understood that the path to success is rarely linear—but always rewarding when guided by values. My time at USC has pushed me to think bigger, speak up, and embrace every opportunity with curiosity. I’m grateful to the mentors, professors, and peers who have helped shape me along the way.
